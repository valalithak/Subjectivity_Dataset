FIFA U-17 World Cup India 2017 in Goa: Schools to reschedule first term exams
With U-17 FIFA world cup matches to be held in Goa from October 7 onwards, the state education minister has recently decided to re-schedule the first terminal examination 2017 in the state.According to reports, this decision has been taken so that the "once in a life time event" of the the Federation Internationale de Football Association (FIFA) matches will not be missed by the students.Here's what Director of Education, G P Bhat said:"FIFA under-17 world cup will be held in India with Goa hosting the some matches from October 7-October 21, 2017 at Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium at Fatorda," State Director of Education, G P Bhat said in a circular addressed to all the schools in the state"In order to enable the students to witness the matches of once in a life time event it is enjoined upon heads of all the schools in the state to re-schedule the first terminal examination so that it gets over on or before October 7, 2017," the circular said. "Regular classes will continue till the end of the term," it added.Furthermore, the state will be hosting several FIFA under-17 matches at the Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium in Fatorda.The matches will be held from October 7 to October 21 this year.Moreover, the circular also read that regular classes will continue till the end of term after FIFA.For more updates on examination dates, all the candidates are advised to keep a track on the official website of Goa education board.(With inputs from PTI) Read: Roza is now an 'infectious disease' in Gujarat school textbook    Read: 'Ugliness is a reason for dowry': Maharashtra Class 12 Sociology textbookRead: What this textbook asked kids to do in an experiment will blow your mind!For more updates, follow India Today Education or you can write to us at education.intoday@gmail.com
